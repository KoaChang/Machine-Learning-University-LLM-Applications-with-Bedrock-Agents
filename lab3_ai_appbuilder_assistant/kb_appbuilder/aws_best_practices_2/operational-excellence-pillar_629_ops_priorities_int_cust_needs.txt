{"page_content": "OPS01-BP02 Evaluate internal customer needs - Operational Excellence PillarOPS01-BP02 Evaluate internal customer needs - Operational Excellence PillarAWSDocumentationAWS Well-ArchitectedAWS Well-Architected FrameworkImplementation guidanceResourcesOPS01-BP02 Evaluate internal customer needs\n    Involve key stakeholders, including business, development, and\n    operations teams, when determining where to focus efforts on\n    internal customer needs. This will ensure that you have a thorough\n    understanding of the operations support that is required to achieve\n    business outcomes.\n  \n    Use your established priorities to focus your improvement efforts\n    where they will have the greatest impact (for example, developing\n    team skills, improving workload performance, reducing costs,\n    automating runbooks, or enhancing monitoring). Update your\n    priorities as needs change.\n  \nCommon anti-patterns:\n\n\n\n        You have decided to change IP address allocations for your\n        product teams, without consulting them, to make managing your\n        network easier. You do not know the impact this will have on\n        your product teams.\n      \n\n\n        You are implementing a new development tool but have not engaged\n        your internal customers to find out if it is needed or if it is\n        compatible with their existing practices.\n      \n\n\n        You are implementing a new monitoring system but have not\n        contacted your internal customers to find out if they have\n        monitoring or reporting needs that should be considered.\n      \n\nBenefits of establishing this best\n    practice: Evaluating and understanding internal customer\n    needs will inform how you prioritize your efforts to deliver\n    business value.\n  \nLevel of risk exposed if this best practice\n    is not established: High\n  \nImplementation guidance\n\n\n\n          Understand business needs: Business success is created by\n          shared goals and understanding across stakeholders including\n          business, development, and operations teams.\n        \n\n\n\n              Review business goals, needs, and priorities of internal\n              customers: Engage key stakeholders, including business,\n              development, and operations teams, to discuss goals,\n              needs, and priorities of internal customers. This ensures\n              that you have a thorough understanding of the operational\n              support that is required to achieve business and customer\n              outcomes.\n            \n\n\n              Establish shared understanding: Establish shared\n              understanding of the business functions of the workload,\n              the roles of each of the teams in operating the workload,\n              and how these factors support shared business goals across\n              internal and external customers.\n            \n\n\nResources\n\nRelated documents:\n\n\n\n\nAWS           Well-Architected Framework Concepts \u00e2\u20ac\u201c Feedback loop\n\n\n Javascript is disabled or is unavailable in your browser.To use the Amazon Web Services Documentation, Javascript must be enabled.
